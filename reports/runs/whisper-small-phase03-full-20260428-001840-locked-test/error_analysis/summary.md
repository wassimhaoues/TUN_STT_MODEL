This report turns prediction-level outputs into concrete failure buckets for follow-up training decisions.

- Predictions CSV: `reports/runs/whisper-small-phase03-full-20260428-001840-locked-test/predictions.csv`

## Overall Metrics

- Corpus WER: `0.488794`

- Short clip threshold: `< 3.0s`

## Main Findings

- Code-switching is still a distinct regime in the test set: average WER is 0.504645 on 595 code-switched samples versus 0.443422 on 441 Arabic-only samples.
- Duration still matters: short clips under 3.0s reach corpus WER 0.414030, while clips at or above 10.0s reach 0.448461.
- A small set of repetition loops is driving the worst failures: 14 samples show repeated-token hallucination, including 9 catastrophic loops with WER above 2.0.
- Major omissions are rare but real: 16 samples collapse into much shorter predictions, often on very short or Latin-heavy references.
- Some errors preserve the rough word skeleton but corrupt token forms: 26 samples land in the high-CER / moderate-WER bucket.
